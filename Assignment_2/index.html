Cash Is (No Longer) King

An exploration of the decling use of cash in Australia

Payments

This chart examines the payment methods used by a representative sample of Australians collected by the RBA.

The steady decline of cash usage brings into question what factors are reinforcing this trend including customer patterns and accessibility and where these changes are seen. This is explored below.

Accessibility

Number of ATMs in Australia

2017-2023

With the usage of cash decreasing, the number of ATMs followed suit. The reducing number of points to withdraw cash has reinforced Australian’s movement to going cashless.

Notes in Circulation by Denomination

2020-2024

COVID-19 led to an interesting increase in the number of high denomination notes in circulation despite a decrease in the use of cash. Suggesting Australian still see value in holding cash in uncertain times.

Customer Patterns

Usage of Cash by Payment Size

2007-2022

While it’s the category where it is used most in presently, the usage of cash has seen its greatest drop in low value payments.

Usage of Cash by Payment Category

2007-2022

Every category of popular payments highlight a decreasing usage of cash, as technology advances to increase accessibility of debit and credit systems for establishments. Interestingly, services has the smallest drop.

Demographics

Now that it has been identified that there is reduced accessibility of cash (despite an increase of notes in circulation) and less usage, it is important to see where it has impacted most.

There is an overall decrease in the number of ATMs per 1000 people across Australia since 2017.

The major cities of WA, SA and Queensland were impacted most, whereas Remote and Very Remote areas of showed the least change indicating a potentially increased prevelance of cash.
